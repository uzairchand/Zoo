Mammals

Lion

The lion is known for its strength and prideful nature, often called the king of the jungle.

Tiger

Tigers are solitary hunters, known for their powerful builds and unique striped coats.

Panda

The giant panda is a gentle creature, mainly found in China, known for its distinctive black and white coat and its diet of bamboo.

Moose

The moose is the largest species in the deer family, found in northern forests. They are solitary animals and excellent swimmers.

Gorilla

Gorillas are gentle giants, mostly found in the forests of Central Africa. They live in groups and are known for their strong family bonds.

Wolf

Wolves are social pack animals known for their intelligence and cooperation during hunts. They are often seen as symbols of wilderness.

Rhinoceros

Rhinos are large, powerful herbivores with thick skin and one or two horns on their snouts. They are critically endangered due to poaching.

Leopard

Leopards are solitary and stealthy predators known for their spotted coats. They are adept climbers and often store their prey in trees

Deer

Deer are graceful herbivores with antlers (in males) that shed and regrow every year. They are found in forests and grasslands worldwide.

Kangaroo

Native to Australia, kangaroos are marsupials known for their powerful hind legs and ability to jump great distances.

Cheetah

Cheetahs are the fastest land animals, capable of reaching speeds up to 60 mph. They are skilled hunters that rely on speed and agility.

Birds

Eagle

Eagles are known for their excellent vision and powerful flight abilities.

Parrot

Parrots are colorful birds known for their intelligence and ability to mimic sounds.

Reptiles

Crocodile

Crocodiles are large aquatic reptiles that have existed for millions of years.

Snake

Snakes are legless reptiles, known for their ability to swallow prey much larger than themselves.
